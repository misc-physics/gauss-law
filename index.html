DEFINITION

Electric flux (Φ) measures how much of an electric field passes through a given surface. Imagine field lines as rain — flux counts how many pass through a tilted net.

①
Uniform field, flat surface
Φ = E · A
E⃗ perpendicular to surface (θ=0°) — maximum flux.
②
Surface tilted at angle θ
Φ = E · A · cos θ
θ is between E⃗ and area normal n̂. As θ→90°, flux→0.
③
General (curved surface)
Φ = ∮ E⃗ · dA⃗
Integrate E⃗ · n̂ dA — foundation of Gauss's Law.
💡 Key insight: Only the component of E⃗ perpendicular to the surface contributes to flux.

Infinite Line Charge

E = λ / (2πε₀r)radially outward · λ = charge/length
Gaussian surface: coaxial cylinder of radius r, length L.
Flux only through curved side; caps = zero.
E ∝ 1/r (not 1/r² like a point charge).
